Losing the Psychological War

Node Link

There recently were allegations of Joss Whedon ("Buffy"/etc.) misconduct against actors on his set. He is not alone as here is a video of George Lucas (Star Wars, Indiana Jones) physically ushering a fellow writer from a conference room due to saying something that wasn't true.

But why are they frustrated? I think it's because more and more screenwriters (including to a large extent the big name directors and producers such as Whedon or Lucas themselves) were touched by the "dark side" of the open / remix trend and started thinking in terms of fanfics of recent franchises and:

  1. Refusing to write in the Hollywood format ( Reference ).

  2. Cannot stand the lack of communication of screenplay readers ( Reference ).

  3. Are frustrated at the prospect of taking the time to avoid de-fanficcing the screenplay ideas from elements of "proprietary" (= restricted, "All rights reserved", "copyrighted")franchises, and reducing the quality of the result. ( Reference ).

Either they get mad at themselves for being so "lazy" or they get mad at the system of "Rules" / laws / court precedents (= the "guidelines"). Either way, they either quit, or it gets much more time consuming and vexing.

The lack of screenplays and screenwriters is the main bottleneck.

But I have a plan forward.

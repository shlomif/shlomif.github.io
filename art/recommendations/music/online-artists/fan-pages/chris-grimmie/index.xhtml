If Ayn Rand was born in the 1990s, she would be Christina Grimmie.

Very much inspired by Peter Ustinov's quote: “If Botticelli were alive today he’d be working for Vogue.” (which was a praise of the then held-in-much-contempt “Vogue” magazine). However, I am taking it a little further. I hope Ms. Grimmie is not offended by me comparing her, who despite her early low-quality cover videos, is almost entirely perfectly normal and wonderful in any way, to Ayn Rand whose words, deeds, and personal life, both exhibited many issues that seem appalling in 2014, and were often quite objectionable even back then.

My point is that both Ayn Rand at the time and Christina Grimmie now, are at the cutting-edge of influencing the world and taking it forward instead of backward (despite whatever faults they both had or have). Back in Ayn Rand’s time books and the early Hollywood feature films were the primary vehicle for influence. In this day and age, however, songs and other videos on YouTube, especially by “independent” cover artists, are what bring the most long-term (and usually positive) influence among the people who matter most (= the “Alphas”).

Sorry for the long explanation.

Chris Grimmie is Alive

I had a similar moment as well.

The Story

"Christina Victoria Grimmie" was a fairly unremarkable name, which her parents chose because they wanted a girl. Nevertheless, she rebelled and announced that she will be a post-modern take on a tomboy: a gamer she-dork, not unlike the early Felicia Day. She also joked that as opposed to her middle name, she is "the biggest loser in history".

While she spent large parts of her early years playing video games, eating and sleeping ( and that's alright with me! ), she eventually started posting covers of pop songs to youtube (possibly out of lethargy or boredom). While many of her early recordings were recorded in a non-professional manner, she has met positive critics and pledges of money and mana. She got a lot of publicity after doing a [high-quality] collaboration with KHS which earned her more money, fame, mana, and friends. ( And that's also fine. )

Around the release of the song "Fireflies", an online friend gave me a link to its KHS cover which I loved and listened to many times. I found it better than the original. Thus, I decided to go on a "Fireflies" youtube covers spree, and ran into Grimmie's cover and for better or for worse said to myself "She's the best!".

Christina Grimmie - not Lady Gaga, Taylor Swift, Katy Perry, Rihannah, or Ariana Grande (all singers with some great songs) - became my new queen of music. The new Madonna if you may.

Given I were a terran terminator, I presume and hope my guardian angels and universal defence/intelligence community (from the NSA and The Mossad to Iran, the PLO, ISIL, to Emma Watson, Melissa Joan Hart, and SMG - I love you all) have boobytrapped my computer, and monitored me. Even if they didn't, I shared my sentiments on publicly accessible sites and services.

As a result, Grimmie became a multiverse superstar. She received even larger pledges and unconditional donations of money and mana. Her face appeared on magazines. She was invited to perform at diverse venues ( including “Gul Dukat Live on Bajor” ), often finding one or more of her soundalikes and lookalikes already on stage (real magic!). She received dividends from unofficial merchandise sales. She likely accepted film roles, where she often kicked ass, including peacefully, but not limited to it. She was offered record deals, but her record sales skyrocketed regardless, despite active "piracy". Finally, just like Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle, she or her lookalikes have accepted modelling gigs (including pornographic ones if those are your cup of tea).

Naturally, she didn't rest on her laurels. She continued to improve her skills as an entertainer. She did collaborations with other musicians (including obscure ones). She also donated money and time to causes she considered noble. Moreover, her bank account credentials were public knowledge (which isn't unusual) so some individuals extracted money out of her account, without hurting her profitability.

(In my defence, I later was impressed by the works of many other YouTube musicians, including of male ones and bands and recommended them, but Ms. Grimmie was always the queen for me.)

However, I didn't stop at that and entertained thoughts of her as an up&coming "hacker queen" of our planet: "If Ayn Rand was born in the 1990s, she would be Christina Grimmie" ( Reference ). As a result, one problem emerged: The-Codex™ explicitly notes that A vampire can only be slain by other vampires, except for the last standing vampire who will be slain by his favourite children.. Grimmie was not a vampire, as she was born a wizard, whom are also known as "true prophets". Moreover, I was not the last vampire standing yet.

Therefore, the game masters who control my media (= "in between") decided, after a fair die roll, to stage Grimmie's death there ( Reference ). Note that she was not really killed. Rather, I was just made to think that.

I also suspect that Christina, many other fellow entertainers / creators, and the universal defence community cooperated reluctantly ( Selina: OK, OK. Tell you what? I’ll play along. ). Nevertheless, the introes and outroes of Grimmie's videos, and also those of other artists I liked (e.g.: Tiffany Alvord ) contain many cuts, likely because they were lying to me.

Letter to Chris

The letter

Dear Chris,

Executive Summary: I have always loved you, I always will, I think you rock (and you are my candidate for being the next second-most-awesome-individual-ever). Moreover, I admit that I believe you are more "able" than I. I need you, I wish you were here, and I strongly suspect you are really still alive, healthy, and kicking.

I may have been overzealous in my tweeting ( https://twitter.com/shlomif/with_replies ) and my "blogging" / web publishing ( https://www.shlomifish.org/art/recommendations/music/online-artists/fan-pages/christina-grimmie/ ) so I apologise for that.

It is a shot in the dark, but if you’re alive, please make it public knowledge. I had a Simba is Alive-like moment ( https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjYGB4uYgWw ) with respect to you (and later to everyone else who reportedly died).

What I taught (and learned from) Internet/social media creators (on YouTube and other Internet outlets)

  1. Be confident - your early work does not have to be perfect.

    Women and girls can also be confident and brave.

  2. You can be both competent and resourceful and socially capable, sexy, and sexually assertive.

  3. Encourage competitors and even give them publicity and advice. ( reference )

  4. Encourage criticism, don't try to please everyone, but realise that you're awesome.

  5. "Make your own kind of music" -

    1. On the other hand, it is OK to build upon, mash up, or even parody others' efforts.

    2. Similarly allow and encourage derivatives.

  6. Be a geek: a neo-amateur - someone who loves his work, but may accept large monetary pledges. ( It will likely yield more money, anyway. )

    1. Ecclesiastes wrote that "reputation is better than fine oil". Around ~2000 Tim O'Reilly noted: The greatest threat to authors and creative artists is not piracy — it’s obscurity..

  7. Don't worry about fans or haters "diluting your brand". Hunting down copyright "violations" is usually a waste of time, energy, and love. Most violators are giving you free publicity anyway.

  8. Be communicative:

    If you do not like a work, please try to comment on why you think it is lacking, rather than completely dismissing it or vaguely criticising it ( e.g: "it sucks"; "you cannot write"; "it's a waste of space"; "try to write it as if you were there" ). People are often amazed by food reviewers in the recently trendy cooking competition ("reality") shows being very pedantic and pinpointing small and large problems, but it comes with experience and "with the territory". So I applaud these reviewers.

  9. Expect that everyone, and everything will improve - the Pygmalion effect vs. the Golem effect.

  10. "When in Doubt Have a Man Come Through a Door with a Gun in His Hand" - keep your creative works short&staccato, but break rules, and be anti-politcorrect. "War is good for business." like The Ferengi Rules of Acquisition go.

  11. If you care enough about a task, then invest time&love in it. There is a difference between hacking and slacking.

Advogato - The second blurb on my user-page.

Looking back at my first Advogato introduction, I cannot believe how my attitude and how much I think and present myself have changed since then, and how much I feel differently about free and open source software (FOSS) and my general enthusiasm about contributing to the digital commons. So I’ll try to summarise it better.

I am Shlomi Fish and I’m an open-source and open-content user (or “consumer” for lack of a better word), creator, enthusiast and advocate, hailing from Tel Aviv, Israel. I originated and/or maintain a lot of open-source software (including many CPAN modules), wrote some articles and essays, authored some material for presentations and talks, and originated some humorous stories, screenplays, aphorisms, UNIX-like fortune cookies, and bits.

Shlomi Fish (שלומי פיש), also known as “Rindolf”, is an Israeli humorist, writer, and software geek.

He is passionate about open content, open source, and freedom and openness in general.

It is easy to reach Shlomi using a large number of online means, including E-mail.
